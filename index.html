Airline Passenger Satisfaction Prediction

This HIT is part of a scientific research project conducted by the University of Texas. Your decision to complete this HIT is voluntary, and your responses are anonymous. The results of the research may be presented at scientific meetings, published in scientific journals, or made publicly available to other researchers. Clicking on the 'Accept' button on the bottom of the page indicates that you are at least 18 years of age, you are a native English speaker, and you agree to complete the HIT voluntarily.

You will be shown a series of airline passenger profiles and survey responses and asked to predict whether passenger was ultimately satisfied or dissatisfied with their flight. You will use information about the passenger's trip (travel class, reason for travel, etc.) and the passenger's survey responses on how they felt about various components of the flight to make your prediction decision. This prediction task is broken up into two stages. You will complete half of the tasks in the first stage. You will then be provided with additional information not available to you in the first stage. You will then be asked to make predictions for the remaining tasks.

What this study is about:
This is a study about identifying which factors and concepts related to airline travel can be used to predict airline passenger satisfaction. The goal is to accurately predict whether airline passengers would be satisfied with their journeys given information about their specific flight experiences.

What we will ask you to do:
This study will be divided into several components. First, you will then be introduced via a tutorial to the airline passenger satisfaction prediction task. The tutorial will describe the prediction goal and all factors/features that will be available to you with definitions. After the tutorial you will be asked to answer a series of questions about the content in the tutorial page to confirm you understood the tutorial. You will then move on to the prediction task during which you will be shown a series of passenger profiles and asked to predict whether the passenger was ultimately satisfied or dissatisfied with their flight. The passenger's ultimate (dis)satisfaction with their flight has a ground truth response that was provided by the passenger but will not be revealed to you until after the end of the experiment. Therefore, there is a correct answer for each task that you will be predicting. After the first round of questions, you will have a short break and will be provided with information on how you did over the first set of questions. You will then be asked to answer another set of questions, just like in the previous round. Finally, you will be asked to answer some more demographic and survey style questions describing your experience with the experiment.

Part 1 Task Instructions

You will be shown a series of airline passenger satisfaction survey responses filled out by airline passengers after they completed a flight. Each passenger rated their satisfaction of several flight related factors on a scale of 1-5 (with 1 being least satisfied and 5 being most satisfied). Along with each passenger's survey responses, you will have access to basic passenger information such as their age, the class they flew in, their airline loyalty status, the amount of time they flew, and whether they experienced delays on the flight. Given this information about each passenger, your task is to predict how the passenger responsed to the question: "How satisfied were you with the flight overall?" Your job is to predict whether each passenger responded to this question with "satisfied" or "dissatisfied." The true overall satisfaction is known but hidden and will be revealed to you once you complete the series of tasks. For each task, if you correctly predict the passenger's satisfaction, you will earn an additional $0.02.

For each passenger, you will have access to the following factors:

Gender: Gender of the passengers (Female, Male)
Customer Type: The customer type (Loyal customer, disloyal customer)
Age: The actual age of the passengers
Type of Travel: Purpose of the flight of the passenger (Personal Travel, Business Travel)
Class: Travel class in the plane of the passenger (Business, Eco, Eco Plus)
Flight distance: The flight distance of this journey
Arrival Delay in Minutes: Minutes delayed when Arrival.
Departure Delay in Minutes: Minutes delayed when departure

You will also have access to the passenger's survey responses indicating their satisfaction regarding the following aspects of their flight:

 Inflight wifi service
 Convenience of Departure/Arrival time
 Ease of Online booking
 Gate location
 Food and drink
 Seat comfort
 Inflight entertainment
 On-board service
 Baggage handling
 Check-in service
 Cleanliness

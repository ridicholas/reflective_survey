Airline Passenger Compensation Plan Decision

This HIT is part of a scientific research project conducted by the University of Texas. Your decision to complete this HIT is voluntary, and your responses are anonymous. The results of the research may be presented at scientific meetings, published in scientific journals, or made publicly available to other researchers. Clicking on the 'Accept' button on the bottom of the page indicates that you are at least 18 years of age, you are able to read and write comfortably in English, and you agree to complete the HIT voluntarily.

You will act as an customer service representative at an airline company and be shown a series of airline passenger profiles and survey responses and asked to decide whether to send the compensation plan for the appropriate customer. You will use information about the passenger's trip (travel class, reason for travel, etc.) and the passenger's survey responses on how they felt about various components of the flight to make your decision. The possible decisions include 1) send cash coupon to the customer; and 2) send word apology to the customer. If the customer is relatively satisfied with the flight, sending the word apology is more appropriate since cash coupon would incur an additional cost. If the customer is dissastified with the flight, sending the cash coupon is more appropriate to improve customer satisfaction.

What this study is about:
This is a study about selecting compensation decisions for airline travel to improve airline passenger satisfaction cost-effectively. The goal is to select which compensation plan would improve overall airline passengers' satisfaction while minimizing the cost of the company (from sending coupons) with their journeys given information about their specific flight experiences.

The passenger's ultimate outcome has a ground truth response but will not be revealed to you. Therefore, there is an optimal decision for each task that you will be predicting.

Task Instructions: Factors

You will be shown a series of airline passenger satisfaction survey responses filled out by airline passengers after they completed a flight. Imagine that you are a customer representative at the airline company and you need to decide which compensation plan to send to the customer. Each passenger rated their satisfaction of several flight related factors on a scale of 1-5 (with 1 being least satisfied and 5 being most satisfied). Along with each passenger's survey responses, you will have access to basic passenger information such as their age, the class they flew in, their airline loyalty status, the amount of time they flew, and whether they experienced delays on the flight. Given this information about each passenger, your task is to select the best compensation plan for the customer. If the customer is really unsatisfied, we can send them cash coupon which has been demonstrated helpful in improving customer satisfaction; however, if the customer is satisfied or not very upset, sending a cash coupon would incur additional cost and is not necessary, the optimal decision would be to just a word apology. The true outcome is known but hidden to you. For each task, if you correctly select the optimal decision, you will earn an additional $0.1.

Factors are pieces of information about each passenger and their flight. For each passenger, you will have access to the following factors:

Gender: Reported gender of the passenger (Female, Male)
Customer Type: The customer type (Loyal customer, disloyal customer). Loyal customers frequently use the same airline and participate in the airline's rewards programs.
Age: The reported age of the passenger.
Type of Travel: Purpose of the flight of the passenger (Personal Travel, Business Travel)
Class: Travel class in the plane of the passenger (Business, Economy, Economy Plus)
Flight distance: What was the distance in kilometers (km) of the passenger's flight? For reference, a flight from New York City to London is 5,567km.
Arrival Delay in Minutes: Minutes flight was delayed from scheduled arrival time.
Departure Delay in Minutes: Minutes flight was delayed from scheduled departure time.

You will also have access to the passenger's survey responses which are the passenger's ratings (1: least satisfied, 5: most satisfied) indicating their satisfaction regarding the following aspects of their flight:

 Inflight wifi service: How satisfied was the passenger with the plane's on board wifi service?
 Convenience of Departure/Arrival time: How satisfied was the passenger with the booked departure and arrival times (Note: this is only for booking time, so it does not consider any delays).
 Ease of online booking: Did the passenger find the online booking process (act of purchasing the flight ticket online) easy and convenient?
 Gate location: Was the passenger satisfied with where their gate was located at the airport?
 Food and drink: Was the passenger satisfied with the food and drink offered on the flight?
 Seat comfort: Did the passenger find their seat on the plane to be comfortable?
 Inflight entertainment: Was the passenger satisfied with the inflight entertainment options on the flight?
 On-board service: Was the passenger satisfied with the level of service offered on the flight?
 Baggage handling: Was the passenger satisfied with how their baggage was handled?
 Check-in service: Did the passenger find the process of checking-in to their flight to be easy and convenient?
 Cleanliness: Did the passenger find the plane interior to be clean?

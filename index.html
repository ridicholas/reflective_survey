Airline Passenger Satisfaction Prediction

This HIT is part of a scientific research project conducted by the University of Texas. Your decision to complete this HIT is voluntary, and your responses are anonymous. The results of the research may be presented at scientific meetings, published in scientific journals, or made publicly available to other researchers. Clicking on the 'Accept' button on the bottom of the page indicates that you are at least 18 years of age, you are able to read and write comfortably in English, and you agree to complete the HIT voluntarily.

You will be shown a series of airline passenger profiles and survey responses and asked to predict whether the passenger was ultimately satisfied or dissatisfied with their flight along with other possible questions. You will use information about the passenger's trip (travel class, reason for travel, etc.) and the passenger's survey responses on how they felt about various components of the flight to make your prediction decision. This prediction task is broken up into two stages. You will complete half of the tasks in the first stage. You will then be provided with additional information not available to you in the first stage. You will then be asked to make predictions for the remaining tasks.

What this study is about:
This is a study about identifying which factors and concepts related to airline travel can be used to predict airline passenger satisfaction. The goal is to accurately predict whether airline passengers would be satisfied with their journeys given information about their specific flight experiences.

What we will ask you to do:
This study will be divided into several components. First, you will then be introduced via a tutorial to the airline passenger satisfaction prediction task. The tutorial will describe the prediction goal and all factors/features that will be available to you with definitions. After the tutorial you will be asked to answer a series of questions about the content in the tutorial page to confirm you understood the tutorial. You will then move on to the prediction task during which you will be shown a series of passenger profiles and asked to predict whether the passenger was ultimately satisfied or dissatisfied with their flight. The passenger's ultimate (dis)satisfaction with their flight has a ground truth response that was provided by the passenger but will not be revealed to you. Therefore, there is a correct answer for each task that you will be predicting. After the first round of questions, you will have a short break and will be provided with information on how you did over the first set of questions. You will then be asked to answer another set of questions, just like in the previous round. Finally, you will be asked to answer some more demographic and survey style questions describing your experience with the experiment.

Part 1 Task Instructions

You will be shown a series of airline passenger satisfaction survey responses filled out by airline passengers after they completed a flight. Each passenger rated their satisfaction of several flight related factors on a scale of 1-5 (with 1 being least satisfied and 5 being most satisfied). Along with each passenger's survey responses, you will have access to basic passenger information such as their age, the class they flew in, their airline loyalty status, the amount of time they flew, and whether they experienced delays on the flight. Given this information about each passenger, your task is to predict how the passenger responsed to the question: "How satisfied were you with the flight overall?" Your job is to predict whether each passenger responded to this question with "satisfied" or "dissatisfied." The true overall satisfaction is known but hidden to you. For each task, if you correctly predict the passenger's satisfaction, you will earn an additional $0.04.

Factors are pieces of information about each passenger and their flight. For each passenger, you will have access to the following factors:

Gender: Reported gender of the passenger (Female, Male)
Customer Type: The customer type (Loyal customer, disloyal customer). Loyal customers frequently use the same airline and participate in the airline's rewards programs.
Age: The reported age of the passenger.
Type of Travel: Purpose of the flight of the passenger (Personal Travel, Business Travel)
Class: Travel class in the plane of the passenger (Business, Economy, Economy Plus)
Flight distance: What was the distance in kilometers (km) of the passenger's flight? For reference, a flight from New York City to London is 5,567km.
Arrival Delay in Minutes: Minutes flight was delayed from scheduled arrival time.
Departure Delay in Minutes: Minutes flight was delayed from scheduled departure time.

You will also have access to the passenger's survey responses which are the passenger's ratings (1: least satisfied, 5: most satisfied) indicating their satisfaction regarding the following aspects of their flight:

 Inflight wifi service: How satisfied was the passenger with the plane's on board wifi service?
 Convenience of Departure/Arrival time: How satisfied was the passenger with the booked departure and arrival times (Note: this is only for booking time, so it does not consider any delays).
 Ease of online booking: Did the passenger find the online booking process (act of purchasing the flight ticket online) easy and convenient?
 Gate location: Was the passenger satisfied with where their gate was located at the airport?
 Food and drink: Was the passenger satisfied with the food and drink offered on the flight?
 Seat comfort: Did the passenger find their seat on the plane to be comfortable?
 Inflight entertainment: Was the passenger satisfied with the inflight entertainment options on the flight?
 On-board service: Was the passenger satisfied with the level of service offered on the flight?
 Baggage handling: Was the passenger satisfied with how their baggage was handled?
 Check-in service: Did the passenger find the process of checking-in to their flight to be easy and convenient?
 Cleanliness: Did the passenger find the plane interior to be clean?

Airline Passenger Satisfaction Prediction

This HIT is part of a scientific research project conducted by the University of Texas. Your decision to complete this HIT is voluntary, and your responses are anonymous. The results of the research may be presented at scientific meetings, published in scientific journals, or made publicly available to other researchers. Clicking on the 'Accept' button on the bottom of the page indicates that you are at least 18 years of age, you are able to read and write comfortably in English, and you agree to complete the HIT voluntarily.

You will be shown a series of airline passenger profiles and survey responses and asked to predict whether the passenger was ultimately satisfied or dissatisfied with their flight along with other possible questions. You will use information about the passenger's trip (travel class, reason for travel, etc.) and the passenger's survey responses on how they felt about various components of the flight to make your prediction decision. This prediction task is broken up into two stages. You will complete half of the tasks in the first stage. You will then be provided with additional information not available to you in the first stage. You will then be asked to make predictions for the remaining tasks.

What this study is about:
This is a study about identifying which factors and concepts related to airline travel can be used to predict airline passenger satisfaction. The goal is to accurately predict whether airline passengers would be satisfied with their journeys given information about their specific flight experiences.

What we will ask you to do:
This study will be divided into several components. First, you will then be introduced via a tutorial to the airline passenger satisfaction prediction task. The tutorial will describe the prediction goal and all factors/features that will be available to you with definitions. After the tutorial you will be asked to answer a series of questions about the content in the tutorial page to confirm you understood the tutorial. You will then move on to the prediction task during which you will be shown a series of passenger profiles and asked to predict whether the passenger was ultimately satisfied or dissatisfied with their flight. The passenger's ultimate (dis)satisfaction with their flight has a ground truth response that was provided by the passenger but will not be revealed to you. Therefore, there is a correct answer for each task that you will be predicting. After the first round of questions, you will have a short break and will be provided with information on how you did over the first set of questions. You will then be asked to answer another set of questions, just like in the previous round. Finally, you will be asked to answer some more demographic and survey style questions describing your experience with the experiment.

Costs/Payments:

You will also be provided with additional incentive compensation dependent on how many prediction tasks you answer correctly, at a rate of $0.05 per correct task answer. There are 50 tasks eligible for the additional incentive bonus, so you can receive a maximum additional incentive compensation of $2.50. You must complete the experiment in its entirety to be eligible for payment. MTurk worker IDs will be collected for the purposes of distributing compensation.

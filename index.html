Airline Passenger Satisfaction Prediction

This HIT is part of a scientific research project conducted by the University of Texas. Your decision to complete this HIT is voluntary, and your responses are anonymous. The results of the research may be presented at scientific meetings, published in scientific journals, or made publicly available to other researchers. Clicking on the 'Accept' button on the bottom of the page indicates that you are at least 18 years of age, you are a native English speaker, and you agree to complete the HIT voluntarily.

You will be shown a series of airline passenger profiles and survey responses and asked to predict whether passenger was ultimately satisfied or dissatisfied with their flight. You will use information about the passenger's trip (travel class, reason for travel, etc.) and the passenger's survey responses on how they felt about various components of the flight to make your prediction decision. This prediction task is broken up into two stages. You will complete half of the tasks in the first stage. You will then be provided with additional information not available to you in the first stage. You will then be asked to make predictions for the remaining tasks.

What this study is about:
This is a study about identifying which factors and concepts related to airline travel can be used to predict airline passenger satisfaction. The goal is to accurately predict whether airline passengers would be satisfied with their journeys given information about their specific flight experiences.

What we will ask you to do:
This study will be divided into several components. The first component will involve answering a series of survey style introductory questions to get a better understanding of your profile and airline experiences. You will then be introduced via a tutorial to the airline passenger satisfaction prediction task. The tutorial will describe the prediction goal, all factors/features that will be available to you with definitions, along with some useful concepts that you may consider. After the tutorial you will be asked to answer a series of questions about the content in the tutorial to confirm you understood the tutorial. You will then move on to the prediction task during which you will be shown passenger profiles and asked to predict whether the passenger was ultimately satisfied or dissatisfied with their flight, along with predicting whether they were satisfied with several specific flight experiences. The passenger's ultimate dis(satisfaction) with their flight has a ground truth response that was provided by the passenger but will not be shown to you until the end of the experiment. Therefore, there is a correct answer for each task that you will be predicting. After the first round of questions, you will have a short break and will be provided with information on how you did over the first set of questions. You will then be asked to answer another set of questions, just like in the previous round. Finally, you will be asked to answer some more survey style questions describing your experience with the experiment.

Part 1 Task Instructions

You will be shown a series of airline passenger satisfaction survey responses filled out by airline passengers after they completed a flight. Each passenger rated their satisfaction of several flight related factors on a scale of 1-5 (with 1 being least satisfied and 5 being most satisfied). Along with each passenger's survey responses, you will have access to basic passenger information such as their age, the class they flew in, their airline loyalty status, the amount of time they flew, and whether they experienced delays on the flight. Given this information about each passenger, your task is to predict what would be the passenger’s response if they were asked "How satisfied were you with the flight overall?" Your job is to predict whether each would respond to this question with "satisfied" or "dissatisfied." The true overall satisfaction is known but hidden and will be revealed to you once you complete the series of tasks. For each task, if you correctly predict the passenger's satisfaction, you will earn an additional $0.02.

Additionally, consider how the factors available come together to form the following concepts: service satisfaction, passenger type, comfort satisfaction, delays. For each passenger, given the information available, rate each of the concepts on a scale of 1-5. Note that there is no ground truth to these concepts. These are just there to help you reason about the task, and there are no right or wrong answers. Each concept can be loosely defined as follows:

Passenger Expectations (1 (Low) - 5 (High)) - Would this passenger have low or high expectations for their flight? What kind of passenger would have low expectations? What kind of passenger would have high expectations? Does the purpose of their trip matter? Does the type of ticket they purchased matter?

In-Flight Experience (1 (Satisfied) - 5 (Unsatisfied)) - Was the passenger satisfied with their experience during the flight (on plane)? What aspects of the satisfaction survey might correspond to the passengers satisfaction during the flight?

Airport Experience (1 (Satisfied) -5 (Unsatisfied)) - Was the passenger satisfied with their experience at the airport? What aspects of the satisfaction survey might correspond to the passengers satisfaction while they are at the airport?

Booking Experience (1 (Satisfied) -5 (Unsatisfied)) - Was the passenger satisfied with the process of booking their flight? What aspects of the satisfaction survey might correspond to the passengers satisfaction while they are booking?

Delays (1 (No Delays) - 5 (Significant Delays)) - Was the passenger's flight significantly delayed?

When solving this task, think about which factors are important to each concept, and think about which factors and concepts are important to an airline passenger's overall flight satisfaction. For each passenger, you will have access to the following factors:

Gender: Gender of the passengers (Female, Male) Customer Type: The customer type (Loyal customer, disloyal customer)
Age: The actual age of the passengers
Type of Travel: Purpose of the flight of the passengers (Personal Travel, Business Travel)
Class: Travel class in the plane of the passengers (Business, Eco, Eco Plus)
Flight distance: The flight distance of this journey
Inflight wifi service: Satisfaction level of the inflight wifi service
Departure/Arrival time convenient: Satisfaction level of Departure/Arrival time convenient
